Recreating Inspirations

DesignEditorialPhotoshop

I found this picture on Instagram — since I'm interested in working on communication, I think working with covers for magazines is an interesting creative step. I studied what the artist did and then recreated my own version from scratch.

It looks like the artist used several layers with different textures, white small text, edits of the subject matter (hand with flower), a marble texture for the title's text background, and a self-made typography. I used these observations as my starting point.

Beverage Brand Identity

DesignBrand IdentityPhotoshop & Illustrator

A full brand identity project for Saint Ci — a Nordic luxury soda. The process started with research into 2024 beverage trends, focusing on luxury positioning and clean aesthetics.

I rendered the bottle by changing a normal bottle picture into a pink bottle, copied it, rotated it 180 degrees and rendered it with opacity settings to create a reflection effect. I then worked on brand attributions, brand values, and a branding map to define the concept and colour direction.

I traced my hand-drawn sketches in Illustrator to create logotypes. I landed on two prototypes, both symbolizing the sailing lifestyle, with an anchor-like "t" in the title so they pair well even when not used in direct association. I went with the first prototype as the biggest trends in the drink industry right now favour a clean aesthetic.

I then used the "warp" function in Photoshop to get a more realistic perspective of the logotype on the product. Finally, I added a background image, extended it using cropping, added the bottle, and used generative AI-filled grapefruit images to give it a realistic campaign look.

Key learning
Layering — merging layers too early meant I couldn't make later changes like removing the outer-glow effect
